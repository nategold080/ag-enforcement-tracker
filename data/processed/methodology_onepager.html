State AG Enforcement Tracker

Methodology & Coverage  |  February 2026

The State AG Enforcement Tracker is a structured, searchable dataset of enforcement actions taken by state Attorneys General offices. It captures who was sued, by which AG, for what violation, under what statute, for how much, and whether it was part of a multi-state coordinated effort — enabling systematic tracking of state-level enforcement risk across industries and jurisdictions.

Data Pipeline

1
Source Collection
Automated scraping of official AG press release archives. Rate-limited, respectful of robots.txt. Raw HTML archived for full auditability.
2
Enforcement Filtering
Two-stage classifier separates enforcement actions from non-enforcement content (advisories, policy statements, personnel announcements). 48% of raw press releases filtered out.
3
Structured Extraction
Rule-based parsing extracts defendants, dollar amounts, dates, statute citations, and action types. Deterministic methods ensure consistency — no LLM variability.
4
Categorization
Keyword-driven taxonomy maps each action to 13 violation categories. Multi-label: a single action can span consumer protection and data privacy simultaneously.
5
Entity Resolution
Company names cleaned, matched against a canonical entity database, and deduplicated — "Juul Labs, Inc." and "JUUL" resolve to the same defendant.
6
Multi-State Linkage
Actions involving the same defendant, similar amounts, and overlapping dates across states are linked as coordinated multi-state enforcement efforts.
7
Quality Scoring
Each record scored 0–1.0 on extraction confidence. Records below threshold flagged for review. Average quality score: 0.70.
